Badieh

After two years of playing together during Michelâ€™s long stay in Iran, a new project was born. Badieh blew a new life into old pieces and dances from Iranian and Afghan Khorasan, a very important geographical area in the history of Persia and the Silk Road. The majority of the chosen pieces by Michel and Mohammad are over 200 years old but, thanks to oral tradition, they are still alive in these borderlands.

In this project, we can hear some of the most essential instruments of Persian Classical music, like the Tar and Setar along with the Arab Oud, the Afghan Rubab and Dutar accompanied by Tombak, Bendhir and Tabla. Some of these instruments are scarcely heard when traditional musicians play this repertory; so without losing authenticity, a different atmosphere is given to these pieces.

The percussionists that recorded the album along Michel and Mohammad are Ramin Ahmadi playing Tabla, Omid Hatami playing Daf, Kuze, Daire and Casure, and Arash Asadi playing the Tombak

Badieh is now mixing and mastering their first album which will be presented in in Iran and Europe after Summer 2020.

Michel and Mohammad will be accompanied in their concerts outside Iran by Parisa Delshad, the Iranian Tombak player residing in Spain.
